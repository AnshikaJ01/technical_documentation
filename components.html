Components of Satellite

Satellites are available in different sizes and shapes but most of the satellites include a minimum of two common parts like an antenna & a power source. The antenna transmits and receives data frequently to Earth whereas the power source is a battery or a solar panel. These panels will make power through changing energy from sunlight to electricity.

Many satellites from NASA include scientific sensors and cameras. In some cases, these devices are connected toward Earth for collecting data from air, water, and land. After that, they connect in the direction of space to gather information about the solar system as well as the universe.

Some other components used in a satellite mainly change based on the function but, some commonly used components are found on most of the satellites like the following.

  • Antennas

    In a satellite system, antennas play a key role in transmitting and receiving signals from Earth.

  • Command & Data Handling

    In a satellite, command as well as control systems check each aspect and get commands for operation from Earth.

  • Guidance & Stabilization

    The position of the satellite can be monitored through sensors to make sure it stays within the accurate orbit & it is sloping in the direction of the right objective. If required, thrusters, as well as other maneuvers, permit a satellite to modify its location and direction.

  • Housing

    It is assembled from tough materials which can survive in the harsh space surroundings.

  • Power

    Most of the satellites depend on a solar array for changing sunlight into electrical energy.

  • Thermal control

    Thermal control protects the equipment of the satellite against severe changes within the temperature.

  • Transponders

    The signals like Uplink as well as downlink will appear and leave at dissimilar frequencies. O, transponders are used to change the frequencies from uplinked to downlink, after that it amplifies the changed transmission for transmitting to Earth.
